SO FAR, SO GOOD

Childhood Years

1 My life began in Tondo, a small district in Manila. 2 Life there was simple, a bit disorderly, but always full of color. 3 I am the eldest among my siblings and cousins, so for a long time I was the only child in our family. 4 Because of that, all the attention and love from my parents, uncles, aunts, and grandparents were centered on me. 5 I grew up surrounded by people who cared for me deeply. 6 As a child, I was joyful, energetic, and full of laughter. 7 I was also a dreamer, believing that nothing was impossible. 8 Looking back, my childhood memories feel very precious. 9 I remember the cold breeze while playing outside without worries. 10 I remember hearing birds chirping with my grandparents’ radio. 11 I remember the excitement every breakfast morning. 12 I remember running through Osorio like it was my playground. 13 I remember playing with friends until sunset. 14 I remember my mother calling me home, sweaty and dirty. 15 I remember waking up early for elementary school. 16 I remember the smell of my grandmother’s cooking. 17 I remember spending ten pesos playing Crossfire. 18 I felt safe, loved, and happy. 19 Life was not perfect, but it felt complete. 20 I remember how happy I truly was back then.

Growing up in Tondo shaped my resilience and appreciation for simple joys.

Teenage Years

1 As time passed, I grew older and matured. 2 Puberty hit, and I learned many life lessons. 3 High school became an unforgettable phase. 4 I met people with different cultures and personalities. 5 Each person taught me something valuable. 6 Socializing became one of my strengths. 7 I learned to understand people better. 8 I discovered hobbies that shaped who I am today. 9 I developed a passion for automotive. 10 I learned to play the guitar and express myself through music. 11 Painting helped me feel calm and focused. 12 Cooking became a creative outlet for me. 13 I explored myself and the world around me. 14 I made mistakes and learned from them. 15 I experienced my first love. 16 I realized love was not like a fairy tale. 17 Senior high introduced me to lifelong friends. 18 I met brothers who became family. 19 I also met my long-term girlfriend. 20 My teenage years were thrilling and unforgettable.

My teenage years built my identity through friendships, music, and discovery.

College Years

1 College life felt exciting and nerve-wracking. 2 I remember my first day walking into campus. 3 Meeting new people was intimidating at first. 4 I eventually found friends I could rely on. 5 Going out with college friends felt refreshing. 6 Choosing my course was a difficult decision. 7 I dreamed of studying aviation. 8 Reality hit when tuition became a problem. 9 I chose Computer Science as my second option. 10 Programming slowly became enjoyable. 11 I achieved personal milestones. 12 I got my first driver’s license. 13 My parents gifted me a motorcycle. 14 It symbolized their sacrifices and support. 15 College taught me responsibility. 16 I learned to manage my time properly. 17 I felt the reality of adulthood. 18 Childhood joy felt farther away. 19 Despite challenges, I treasured the experiences. 20 College helped me grow into who I am today.

Computer Science challenged me to think logically and grow independently.
